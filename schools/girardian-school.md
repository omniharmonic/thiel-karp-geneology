# The Girardian School

The intellectual tradition growing out of [René Girard](../people/rene-girard.md)'s work on [mimetic theory](../concepts/mimetic-theory.md), the [scapegoat mechanism](../concepts/scapegoat-mechanism.md), and the Judeo-Christian revelation of sacred violence. Less an academic "school" in the formal institutional sense than a trans-disciplinary research program held together by shared theoretical commitments.

## Institutional expressions

- **Colloquium on Violence and Religion (COV&R)** — the principal academic society, founded 1990; holds annual meetings; publishes *Contagion: Journal of Violence, Mimesis, and Culture*.
- **Imitatio** — foundation established in 2007 with funding from [Peter Thiel](../people/peter-thiel.md) to support Girardian research. Imitatio has funded conferences, publications, and early-career scholarships in the Girardian tradition.
- **Stanford Bass Seminar** and related Stanford programs during Girard's lifetime.

## Major figures and lineages

### Girard's close collaborators

- **Jean-Michel Oughourlian** — psychiatrist; co-author of *Things Hidden* interviews
- **Guy Lefort** — co-editor of *Things Hidden*
- **Raymund Schwager** — Catholic theologian, first major theological reader of Girard
- **Robert Hamerton-Kelly** — Stanford Methodist minister, organizer of Girardian seminars (including the one where Thiel's *Straussian Moment* first appeared)

### Catholic / theological inheritors

- **James Alison** — British Catholic priest and the most accomplished English-language Girardian theologian
- **Gil Bailie** — author of *Violence Unveiled*
- **Michael Kirwan, SJ** — British Jesuit Girardian

### Anthropological / philosophical

- **Eric Gans** — Girard's student at Buffalo; founded *Generative Anthropology* as a revision of mimetic theory, placing the "originary event" of language at the center.
- **Andrew McKenna** — literary scholar; Girardian readings of modern literature and deconstruction.

### Cultural and popular

- **[Peter Thiel](../people/peter-thiel.md)** — Girard's most famous business-world student
- Various Silicon Valley readers who have popularized elements of mimetic theory (Luke Burgis's *Wanting* is the most prominent recent example)
- Contemporary political commentators who use the scapegoat mechanism to read social media dynamics

## Distinctive commitments

- **Mimetic desire** as the fundamental structure of human consciousness and conflict
- **Sacred violence** as the structural origin of religion, culture, and law
- **The Hebrew Bible and Gospels** as the unique textual tradition that reveals and thereby dismantles the scapegoat mechanism
- **Anti-Nietzscheanism** — Girard thought [Nietzsche](../people/friedrich-nietzsche.md) was right about what Christianity had done and wrong to lament it
- **Anti-sacrificial Christianity** — the Cross as the exposure, not the repetition, of sacrificial violence

## Tensions and debates

Internal debates within Girardian scholarship include:

- Whether mimetic theory is a general anthropology or a more limited hypothesis about specific phenomena
- How strong Girard's claim about the uniqueness of the Christian revelation really is
- Whether Girard's late "apocalyptic" turn extends the theory or departs from it
- How mimetic theory relates to other contemporary frameworks: [Freud](../people/sigmund-freud.md), evolutionary psychology, cognitive science

## Relevance to this vault

The Girardian school is the primary intellectual infrastructure around [Thiel](../people/peter-thiel.md)'s most-cited influence. Thiel is not merely a reader of Girard but a patron of the Girardian research program through Imitatio. His own public work (*Zero to One*, interviews, essays) has functioned as the most effective popular vector for Girardian theory in the 21st century, for better and for worse.
