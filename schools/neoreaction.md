# Neoreaction (NRx) and the Dark Enlightenment

A loose, internet-native political-philosophical tendency that emerged in the late 2000s — initially through the anonymous blogging of [Curtis Yarvin](../people/curtis-yarvin.md) as *Mencius Moldbug* (2007–2014) — and was theoretically codified by [Nick Land](../people/nick-land.md)'s long essay *The Dark Enlightenment* (2012).

Not a formal school. More a diffuse set of interlinked claims, hosted initially on blogs and Twitter, that has moved steadily into mainstream American political discourse in the 2020s via Silicon Valley and the Vance-adjacent right.

## Core claims

- **Democracy is dysfunctional.** Its apparent outcomes are produced by an unelected permanent class (Yarvin's "Cathedral" — universities, NGOs, press, permanent bureaucracy) that sets ideological direction while electoral politics provides theater.
- **The Cathedral is a soft-theocracy.** A distributed ideological mechanism, not a conspiracy but a self-reinforcing network whose orthodoxy is progressive and whose function is managerial.
- **Exit, not voice.** Reform is impossible; small, competitive, exit-available polities ("patchwork") are the only viable trajectory. This connects neoreaction to crypto and to network-state projects (Balaji Srinivasan).
- **Sovereignty as the core concept.** Via Schmitt: a polity needs a sovereign who can decide; liberal democracies' distributed authority is incoherent.
- **CEO-as-sovereign.** The well-run corporation is the model. Yarvin's *Patchwork* imagines joint-stock polities.
- **Accelerationism (Land variant).** Capital is the intelligence-producing process; human political control over it is both futile and counter-productive.
- **Selective anti-egalitarianism.** A willingness to countenance hereditary, biological, or cultural differences as politically relevant in ways mainstream liberalism rules out in advance.

## Intellectual influences (what NRx cites)

- [Schmitt](../people/carl-schmitt.md) — sovereignty, critique of liberalism
- Thomas Carlyle — on heroes and strong leadership (especially Moldbug)
- James Burnham (*The Managerial Revolution*) — on the managerial class
- Austrian economics (Hans-Hermann Hoppe especially) — libertarian substrate
- [Nietzsche](../people/friedrich-nietzsche.md), [Heidegger](../people/martin-heidegger.md) — ambient background
- Cybernetics and systems theory — especially in Land
- Science-fiction and internet culture — a real influence on the aesthetic register

## Key figures

- [Curtis Yarvin](../people/curtis-yarvin.md) / Moldbug — founder
- [Nick Land](../people/nick-land.md) — theoretical codifier via *The Dark Enlightenment*
- Hans-Hermann Hoppe — *Democracy: The God That Failed* (2001) is a key text
- Bronze Age Pervert (BAP) — adjacent Nietzschean popularizer
- Various bloggers and pseudonymous writers

## Relationship to contemporary politics

In 2025–2026 the influence of NRx-adjacent thinking is more visible in American political life than at any previous point:

- **J.D. Vance** has explicitly cited Yarvin
- **Elon Musk** and the DOGE phase of 2025 shared Moldbug-style structural critiques of the federal bureaucracy
- **[Thiel](../people/peter-thiel.md)** has hosted, funded, and occasionally publicly endorsed figures in this orbit
- The network-state project (Balaji Srinivasan) operates in a similar design space

This does not mean mainstream American politics *is* neoreaction. It does mean that vocabulary and argumentative structures once confined to 2010s blogs are now audible in senior political speech.

## Critical response

From the left: NRx is read as a philosophically dressed-up authoritarianism with pseudo-scientific biological-realist tendencies, selective in its history, and uncommitted to the democratic norms its rhetoric requires it to defeat.

From the traditional right: NRx is read as insufficiently grounded in lived tradition, religion, or national belonging — a hyper-rationalist anti-liberalism that shares too many of liberalism's bad assumptions.

From the Habermasian/Karp side: NRx is the most vivid contemporary expression of exactly the anti-democratic decisionism that post-war critical theory was built to refute.

## Relevance to this vault

On the [Thiel](../people/peter-thiel.md) side only. Karp's Habermasian formation puts him on the opposite pole of 20th- and 21st-century political thought. The Palantir collaboration thus has an under-discussed tension: one of its founders is sympathetic to a tradition that wants to end democratic politics as currently structured; the other is formally trained in the tradition of its most sophisticated defense.
