New rhythm: 4-week-out booking, post-assembly summaries, monthly pulses + field notes</h3><p>In the May 4 weekly sync, Patricia and Benjamin moved the Assembly cadence from one-off to predictable — booking four sessions in advance, publishing a summary after each one <em>“properly starting tomorrow,”</em> committing to one Field Note per month and three Pulses across April–May–June. The <strong>Hive</strong> is the <em>infrastructure</em> the Swarm populates and refines — the persistent substrate where contributors actually scout, garden, connect, compose, and harvest. The Swarm has demand-side traction (registrations are real). The Hive needs builders.</p><blockquote><p><strong>Note on language:</strong> the word “Hive” hasn’t appeared in prior calls — tonight is its first network-facing use. Anchor it in what people already know: the canvas, the toolkit, the microsite, the opencivic-system scaffolding. It’s a clarifying name for the thing we’ve been circling, not a new thing competing with the Swarm.</p></blockquote><h3>What the Hive is — working definition</h3><ul><li><p>An <strong>opencivic-system scaffolding</strong> — defined functional requirements that participants populate by surfacing existing work and stitching new contributions together.</p></li><li><p>A <strong>two-sided microsite</strong> (enrollment + participation) plus a <strong>scrolling-canvas knowledge commons map</strong> of modular components.</p></li><li><p>A <strong>utility-types taxonomy</strong> — ontology engines, curation protocols, knowledge graphs, interoperability layers, an Assembly Wizard, a Component Index, Playbooks.</p></li><li><p><strong>Federated and stigmergic</strong> by design — built on Darren Zal’s COI-protocol pattern: overlapping commons that signal to each other rather than centralizing.</p></li><li><p>Tagline: <em>“Break the silo, join the commons.”</em></p></li></ul><h3>Why it matters now</h3><ul><li><p>It’s the <strong>operational substrate of Phase 02.</strong> The new Citizen/Contributor membership model launched Feb 17 — but “Contributor with knowledge-commons edit access after training” is meaningless without a commons substantial enough to contribute to.</p></li><li><p><code>commons.opencivics.co</code> is <strong>migrating off Notion.</strong> Spencer is researching alternatives. The Hive fills that gap.</p></li><li><p>A demonstrable, federated knowledge commons is the <strong>visible artifact</strong> that makes the BFI / multi-month-contract funding case.</p></li></ul><h3>Where help would land — six specific ways to plug in</h3><ol><li><p><strong>Front-end / web developer</strong> — ship the two-sided microsite and the interactive canvas/map. Swarm-blocking dependency.</p></li><li><p><strong>Bioregional / domain stewards</strong> — scout existing components from your community into the canvas and run a domain alliance rather than reinventing one (bioregional commoning, regen finance, civic tech). Citizen-/Contributor-level on-ramp; if you already gather aligned people, this is yours.</p></li><li><p><strong>Temporal-ontology contributor</strong> — CdV Saizan asked at the Feb 17 Assembly: <em>“how does knowledge that’s only relevant in summer surface in summer?”</em> Unowned.
