# Research Plan + Methodology

## The research question

What is the full constellation of intellectual influences that shaped Peter Thiel and Alex Karp, and why does their philosophical disagreement hold the shape it does?

## What "deep enough" means

We go **two rings out** from the primary subjects:

- **Ring 0:** Thiel, Karp
- **Ring 1:** Their directly-attested teachers and intellectual heroes (people they have cited in print or lectures, or studied under in university)
- **Ring 2:** The teachers, interlocutors, and immediate intellectual context of Ring 1
- **Ring 3 (selective):** Historical anchors that Ring 2 figures built upon — included only where the through-line runs directly to Thiel or Karp

Ring 3 is filtered. We don't need a full history of Western philosophy. We need Plato because Strauss read him a certain way; we need Hegel because Kojève read him a certain way and that reading flowed into both Thiel (via Girard) and Karp (via Habermas).

## Evidentiary standard

Every attributed influence is traceable to at least one of:

1. **Direct citation** — Thiel or Karp has named the figure in writing or recorded speech
2. **Academic lineage** — formal teacher-student relationship
3. **Institutional context** — attended a lecture series, department, or formative event structured around the figure
4. **Textual dependency** — work that cannot be read without the prior work (e.g., Strauss on Plato)

Where a link is speculative or reconstructed, the note says so explicitly.

## Structure of individual notes

Each person-profile follows this skeleton:

1. **One-line identity** — discipline, era, national tradition
2. **Brief bio** — life and career in ~150 words
3. **Core philosophical contributions** — what they're actually known for
4. **Key works** — canonical texts with year
5. **Influenced by** — upstream (with wikilinks)
6. **Influenced** — downstream (with wikilinks)
7. **Relevance to Thiel / Karp** — direct line back to the subjects
8. **Quotes / key passages** — when illuminating
9. **Tags**

## Direct-attestation anchors

These are the sourced claims the vault is built on:

### Thiel

- **Girard** — Thiel took Girard's courses at Stanford as an undergraduate; has named Girard as his most important intellectual influence repeatedly in interviews and lectures. See Thiel's Stanford CS183 / Blake Masters notes, his 2004 piece "The Straussian Moment," and his introduction to "Mimesis and Science" (Girard Festschrift).
- **Strauss** — "The Straussian Moment" is itself a Straussian essay. Thiel read Strauss via Allan Bloom's circle of students and the Hoover Institution context at Stanford.
- **Schmitt** — cited in "The Straussian Moment" as a key diagnostician of modern political theology; Thiel has extended this reading in multiple lectures.
- **Bloom, Rand, Friedman, Hayek** — Thiel's Stanford undergraduate years, founding of *The Stanford Review*, early libertarian activism.
- **Tolkien** — Palantir, Valar Ventures, Mithril Capital, Rivendell — naming pattern is deliberate.
- **Yarvin, Land** — ongoing intellectual engagement; Thiel has funded or hosted events connected to neoreaction.
- **Christianity / Girardian reading of the Gospels** — Thiel has explicitly framed his faith in Girardian terms.

### Karp

- **Habermas** — Karp's doctoral supervisor at Goethe University Frankfurt; dissertation was "Aggression in the Life-World" (German: *Aggression in der Lebenswelt*).
- **Apel** — on dissertation committee / second reader; Karp's engagement with communicative ethics runs through Apel as well.
- **Frankfurt School (Adorno, Horkheimer, Marcuse, Benjamin)** — the institutional and intellectual context of Karp's PhD.
- **Stanford Law** — shared context with Thiel; formal legal training.

### Shared

- **Stanford Law (1990–92 cohort)** — the meeting point. Both men moved through the same institution at the same moment, which was also home to an active Straussian/Hoover conservative scene and, on the law side, to the emerging law-and-economics movement.

## Out-of-scope for this vault

- Individual biographical gossip about Thiel or Karp (affairs, personal finance, etc.)
- Day-to-day operational history of Palantir
- Detailed technical architecture of Palantir products
- Most current political reporting (cited only where needed)

## Success criteria

The vault is done when:

1. Every direct influence named in public by Thiel or Karp has a profile
2. Every Tier 1 profile's own top 3 influences have profiles
3. The 7 major schools/traditions are characterized
4. The 10 load-bearing concepts are explained
5. The synthesis essays make the full constellation legible
6. The wikilink graph is dense enough that you can walk from Thiel to Marx via at least three distinct paths
