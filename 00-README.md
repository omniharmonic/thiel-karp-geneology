# Thiel вЂ“ Karp Intellectual Genealogy

*A constellation map of the thinkers, traditions, and concepts that shaped Peter Thiel and Alex Karp вЂ” the Stanford Law friends who co-founded Palantir from opposite ends of 20th-century political philosophy.*

## Why this exists

Peter Thiel and Alex Karp met at Stanford Law School in the early 1990s. They disagreed about almost everything and became lifelong friends. Twelve years later they co-founded **Palantir** вЂ” a company whose name comes from Tolkien, whose intellectual DNA draws from a German doctoral dissertation written under JГјrgen Habermas, and whose political posture owes more to Leo Strauss and Carl Schmitt than to Silicon Valley's usual pantheon.

This vault traces the full intellectual genealogy. Not the summary. The actual constellation of thinkers, lineages, concepts, and traditions that each man absorbed, argued with, and carries forward вЂ” two rings out from the primary subjects, because the interesting patterns only appear at that distance.

The central puzzle: **how did two men from philosophical traditions that were designed to refute each other end up building the same company?**

- **Peter Thiel** comes out of a distinctly American West Coast Straussianism, fused with RenГ© Girard's mimetic theory (learned directly from Girard as an undergraduate), Carl Schmitt's political theology, Allan Bloom's Nietzsche-tinged conservatism, and the libertarian tradition of Hayek, Friedman, and (in his early years) Rand. Overlaid on this is a Christian theological commitment that he explicitly reads through Girard.

- **Alex Karp** earned his PhD in *Neoclassical Social Theory* from Goethe University Frankfurt under **JГјrgen Habermas** and **Karl-Otto Apel** вЂ” the two major inheritors of the Frankfurt School's critical theory program. His philosophical formation is the direct lineage Thiel's heroes spent careers attacking.

These two traditions are not just different. They are antipodal. Strauss wrote against the Frankfurt School. Schmitt was Habermas's recurring intellectual antagonist. Girard's anthropology is incompatible with Habermas's communicative rationality. Yet Thiel and Karp's friendship вЂ” and Palantir вЂ” holds both in tension.

This research project maps that tension by tracing the full constellation.
