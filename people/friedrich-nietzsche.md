# Friedrich Nietzsche

**(1844–1900)**

German philosopher. Classical philologist turned cultural diagnostician, then madman. The 19th-century figure whose ghost haunts both Thiel's and Karp's lineages — one of the three [shared upstream ancestors](../synthesis/shared-upstream-ancestors.md) (with [Hegel](gwf-hegel.md) and [Heidegger](martin-heidegger.md)) whose work is metabolized very differently by each tradition.

---

## Biographical sketch

Born in Röcken, Saxony; son of a Lutheran pastor. Prodigy philologist; professor of classical philology at Basel at 24. Friendship and break with Richard Wagner. Resigned his chair in 1879 due to ill health. Wrote his mature works (from *The Gay Science* through *Twilight of the Idols*) in a ten-year peripatetic period of extraordinary productivity. Mental collapse in Turin, 1889; final eleven years in his sister's custody, silent and unaware.

## Core contributions

### The genealogy of morality

Western morality, per Nietzsche, is not a timeless deliverance of reason but a historical product — specifically, the revolt of the slave against the master. Slave morality invents the categories of "evil" (to name what the master is) and "good" (to name what the slave is, re-described); it is a project of resentment turned into values. Christianity completes this revolt. The Enlightenment secularizes it. Modern egalitarianism, pity, democracy, socialism, and the critique of strong will are, for Nietzsche, its surviving forms.

### The death of God and nihilism

God is dead; we have killed him; we have not yet understood what this means. Secular modernity pretends to have dispensed with God while retaining the moral structures that only made sense with Him. The result, as this incoherence works itself out, is [nihilism](../concepts/will-to-power.md): the moment when the highest values devalue themselves.

### Will to power

Nietzsche's positive metaphysical hypothesis: the fundamental drive of all living things is not self-preservation (Darwin) or pleasure (utilitarians) but the striving for greater power, expansion, self-overcoming. Every interpretation, value, and institution is a crystallization of will to power. See [will-to-power](../concepts/will-to-power.md).

### Eternal recurrence and the Übermensch

The thought experiment: what if this same life, down to the smallest detail, were to recur eternally? The figure who can say yes to this — who can affirm existence without Christian-moral comfort — is the Übermensch, the overman. This is Nietzsche's image of a post-nihilist life.

### Perspectivism and genealogy as method

There are no facts, only interpretations. The work of philosophy is therefore genealogical: trace the historical origins of concepts and values, expose their underlying drives and struggles. This method shapes 20th-century thought on both the right (Heidegger, Foucault on the left) and in critical theory generally.

## Influenced by

- Arthur Schopenhauer — early formative influence; Nietzsche's later work is partly a working-out of the break
- Richard Wagner — friendship, collaboration, and break
- The Greek tragedians, Pre-Socratics, Heraclitus — Nietzsche's ideal philosophers
- Ralph Waldo Emerson — read throughout life; the American sage Nietzsche could love
- [Hegel](gwf-hegel.md) — antagonistic absorption; the system-builder Nietzsche defines himself against

## Influenced

- [Heidegger](martin-heidegger.md) — devoted lectures and published volumes to Nietzsche; read him as the last metaphysician
- Sigmund Freud — admitted having avoided reading Nietzsche to preserve his own originality
- [Girard](rene-girard.md) — calls Nietzsche the most profound of the anti-Christians; reads him as the thinker who saw what the Gospels reversed
- [Adorno](theodor-adorno.md), [Horkheimer](max-horkheimer.md) — *Dialectic of Enlightenment* reads Nietzsche as diagnostician of instrumental reason
- [Strauss](leo-strauss.md) — Nietzsche as diagnostician of modernity's third wave
- [Bloom](allan-bloom.md) — *The Closing of the American Mind* is a Straussian reading of Nietzsche's arrival in the American academy
- Michel Foucault, Gilles Deleuze, Jacques Derrida — French Nietzsche
- [Rand](ayn-rand.md) — early influence she later disavowed
- [Thiel](peter-thiel.md) — multiply mediated (via Girard, Bloom, and direct reading)

## The forked Nietzsche

One of the most important facts about the 20th-century Nietzsche is that he was claimed by both right and left, and by both Thiel's and Karp's traditions — but read differently.

**Thiel's Nietzsche** (via Bloom and Strauss): diagnostician of nihilism, prophet of the exhaustion of egalitarian morality, the thinker who saw that modernity could not sustain itself. The figure whose *will to power* authorizes founder ambition and monopoly. But because Thiel also reads through Girard, his Nietzsche is a doomed figure — the one who saw the Gospel reversal and couldn't accept it.

**Karp's Nietzsche** (via the Frankfurt School): diagnostician of instrumental reason, of the hidden violence in modern rationalization, of the slave morality that infects bourgeois culture. But the Frankfurt School Nietzsche is a *resource* for critique, not a model for conduct; they absorb his diagnostic tools while rejecting his aristocratic politics.

The two traditions end up agreeing on the diagnosis and disagreeing on the therapy.

## Relevance to Thiel / Karp

See [shared-upstream-ancestors](../synthesis/shared-upstream-ancestors.md) for the full argument.
