# Thomas Hobbes

**(1588–1679)**

English political philosopher. Author of *Leviathan* (1651), the founding text of modern political philosophy on most Straussian accounts.

## Core claims

- **State of nature:** life without political authority is "solitary, poor, nasty, brutish, and short" — a war of all against all.
- **Social contract:** individuals rationally agree to transfer their natural right of self-defense to a sovereign who monopolizes legitimate force.
- **Sovereign as mortal god:** the sovereign (the Leviathan) is whatever body makes the decisive judgment on disputes; its authority is grounded not in divine right but in rational consent and the need for peace.
- **Materialist philosophy:** motion, mechanism, causation — no final causes, no substantial forms.

## Influence on this vault

- **For Strauss:** Hobbes is modernity's founding move — the "lowering of the horizon" from the classical concern with virtue to the modern concern with security and peace. Strauss takes this as a decisive loss. Thiel inherits that diagnosis.
- **For [Schmitt](carl-schmitt.md):** *The Leviathan in the State Theory of Thomas Hobbes* (1938) is a whole book on Hobbes. Schmitt reads Hobbes as inaugurating state sovereignty but also as containing the seed of liberalism's eventual self-dissolution.
- **For [Habermas](jurgen-habermas.md):** Hobbes represents the strategic-action paradigm that communicative-action theory is designed to supplement (not refute).
