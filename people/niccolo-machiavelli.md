# Niccolò Machiavelli

**(1469–1527)**

Florentine diplomat, political theorist, and playwright. Author of *The Prince* (1513) and the *Discourses on Livy* (1517).

## Core claims

- **Virtù and fortuna:** the political actor's skillful engagement with circumstance, as against the fortune or luck that history throws. The great prince improvises toward outcomes in an uncertain world.
- **Separation of politics from morality:** the prince's duty is to the state's survival and glory, and this may require actions that violate conventional morality.
- **Republicanism:** in the *Discourses*, Machiavelli is a republican theorist — free cities with institutionalized conflict (between the few and the many) are the healthiest.

## Influence on this vault

- **For Strauss:** Machiavelli is the founding criminal of modernity. *Thoughts on Machiavelli* (1958) reads Machiavelli as the conscious, deliberate inaugurator of modern political thought's break with classical and Christian teaching. Thiel inherits this framing.
- **For [Schmitt](carl-schmitt.md):** Machiavelli is the theorist of the political as distinct from the moral, which Schmitt will generalize into friend/enemy.
- **For republican traditions** (including those Karp's Habermas finally integrates): Machiavelli the republican theorist of citizen virtue and institutional conflict.
