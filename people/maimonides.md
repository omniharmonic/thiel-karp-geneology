# Maimonides

**(Moses ben Maimon / Rambam, 1138–1204)**

Medieval Jewish philosopher, physician, and rabbinical authority. Author of *The Guide of the Perplexed* (c. 1190) — the greatest medieval Jewish philosophical work — and the *Mishneh Torah* code of Jewish law.

## Relevance to this vault

Maimonides is [Strauss](leo-strauss.md)'s lifelong philosophical touchstone. Strauss's entire hermeneutic doctrine of [esoteric writing](../concepts/esoteric-writing.md) was developed first in the context of reading Maimonides, who in the *Guide* openly discusses how a philosophical author conceals dangerous teachings within pious surfaces.

For Strauss, Maimonides is the exemplary case of a philosopher working inside a revealed religion who preserves the philosophical tradition through careful, hidden writing. This is the model for the Straussian approach to the entire tradition — Plato, Xenophon, Alfarabi, Averroes, and Maimonides as practitioners of the art of philosophical communication under political-theological constraint.

Thiel inherits this Straussian Maimonides indirectly: the assumption that serious texts are written esoterically is a Maimonidean assumption transmitted through Strauss.
