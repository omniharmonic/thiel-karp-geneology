# Ayn Rand

**(Alisa Zinovyevna Rosenbaum, 1905–1982)**

Russian-American novelist and self-styled philosopher. Architect of *Objectivism*, a rationalist-egoist philosophical system articulated across novels (*The Fountainhead*, 1943; *Atlas Shrugged*, 1957) and non-fiction essays (*The Virtue of Selfishness*, 1964; *Capitalism: The Unknown Ideal*, 1966). The most consequential mid-20th-century American popular ideologist of libertarian capitalism and heroic individualism.

## Core claims

- **Reality exists independently of consciousness** (metaphysical realism); **reason is the only means of knowledge**; **the self is the proper moral beneficiary of one's actions** (rational egoism); **laissez-faire capitalism is the only moral economic system**; **the initiation of physical force is evil**.
- **Productive achievement** (the entrepreneur, the architect, the artist) is the highest moral activity.
- **Altruism** — understood as the demand that one subordinate one's life to others — is the root of collectivist evil.

## Influences

- Aristotle — her self-declared philosophical ancestor
- [Nietzsche](friedrich-nietzsche.md) — early heavy influence; later disavowed
- Victor Hugo, Dostoevsky — literary influences

## Influenced

- Alan Greenspan, Ron Paul, and a whole stratum of American libertarian politicians
- The Silicon Valley "founder-hero" mythos
- [Thiel](peter-thiel.md) — youthful Rand reader; later explicit distancing

## Relevance to Thiel

Early influence in Thiel's undergraduate libertarian period. Thiel has since explicitly distanced himself from Rand, partly because his Christian turn made her atheism untenable, and partly because his Girardian sensibilities are incompatible with Rand's thick individualism. The trace remains in the tech-founder-as-hero frame that Thiel uses and that ultimately derives from her.
