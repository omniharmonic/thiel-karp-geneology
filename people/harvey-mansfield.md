# Harvey Mansfield

**(Harvey Claflin Mansfield Jr., b. 1932)**

American political philosopher. Long-tenured Harvard professor (William R. Kenan Jr. Professor of Government). One of the most influential living American Straussians and, among that group, probably the most publicly visible.

---

## Intellectual position

Mansfield occupies a distinctive spot in the American Straussian landscape. Chicago-trained (PhD, 1961) but Harvard-based for his entire career, he sits between the East Coast and Chicago wings of [Straussianism](../schools/straussianism.md). More willing than the East Coast wing to engage with contemporary American politics; more textually rigorous than some of the West Coast wing's polemical mode.

His scholarly work includes:

- Translations and commentaries on [Machiavelli](niccolo-machiavelli.md) — *The Prince* (1985), the *Discourses on Livy* (1996, with Nathan Tarcov)
- Work on Tocqueville — with Delba Winthrop, a translation of *Democracy in America* (2000) that has become the standard American scholarly edition
- *Manliness* (2006) — Mansfield's most public-facing book, a philosophical treatment of the virtue of manliness in a post-feminist democratic society

Mansfield's Machiavelli is a philosophically sophisticated reader of classical political philosophy who deliberately breaks with it — echoing [Strauss](leo-strauss.md)'s *Thoughts on Machiavelli* but with different emphases.

## Relevance to this vault

Mansfield is part of the American Straussian milieu that shaped the intellectual climate of [Thiel](peter-thiel.md)'s undergraduate and post-graduate years. While he did not teach Thiel directly, he is one of the intellectual figures of the Americanized Straussian tradition whose influence flows through the broader conservative intellectual infrastructure (the *Claremont Review of Books*, First Things, the Hoover circle) that Thiel inhabits.

Mansfield is also notable for having taught a generation of American conservative intellectuals, including William Kristol, Clifford Orwin, Andrew Sullivan, Alan Keyes, and (more peripherally) several figures in the post-2016 American right.
