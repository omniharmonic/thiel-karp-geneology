# Edmund Husserl

**(1859–1938)**

German philosopher. Founder of phenomenology. Teacher of [Heidegger](martin-heidegger.md). The origin of the concept of *Lebenswelt* (life-world) that Habermas made central and that appears in the title of Karp's dissertation.

## Core contributions

- **Phenomenology** as a rigorous science of the structures of consciousness. "To the things themselves!"
- **Intentionality:** consciousness is always consciousness of something; there is no disembodied observer.
- **The *Lebenswelt* (life-world):** in Husserl's late work, the pre-theoretical world of ordinary experience that underlies all scientific abstraction. Science's truths are true, but they are founded on a life-world they presuppose and cannot explain away.

## Relevance to Karp

Directly via the title of Karp's dissertation, *Aggression in der Lebenswelt*. The life-world concept enters Habermas (*Theory of Communicative Action*) as the sphere of communicative action, in contrast to the "systems" of money and administrative power. Karp inherits this full architecture.

## Relevance to Thiel

Indirect. But Husserlian phenomenology is also the ground of Heidegger's *Being and Time*, which shaped Strauss's early formation; so the phenomenological lineage flows into both sides of this genealogy.
