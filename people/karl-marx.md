# Karl Marx

**(1818–1883)**

German philosopher, economist, and revolutionary. The thinker without whom the 20th century is illegible — and, via the Frankfurt School, a deep upstream influence on the Karp side of this genealogy.

## Core contributions

- **Historical materialism:** history is the history of class struggle, and the material base (forces and relations of production) is the causally primary factor; ideas, institutions, law, and culture are superstructure.
- **Theory of capital:** value is produced by labor; surplus value is the source of profit; capitalism is characterized by exploitation, alienation, commodity fetishism, tendencies toward monopoly and crisis.
- **Alienation:** modern labor alienates the worker from the product, from the process, from other workers, and from species-being.
- **Ideology critique:** dominant ideas are the ideas of the dominant class; unmasking this is the work of critique.

## Works

- *Economic and Philosophic Manuscripts* (1844)
- *The German Ideology* (1846, with Engels)
- *Communist Manifesto* (1848, with Engels)
- *Grundrisse* (1857–1858)
- *Capital, vol. 1* (1867)

## Influence on this vault

- Directly to the [Frankfurt School](../schools/frankfurt-school.md) and thus to Karp via Habermas, Adorno, Horkheimer, Marcuse, Benjamin.
- Indirectly to Thiel's tradition: Strauss, Schmitt, and the Straussian conservatives all defined themselves against Marxism; the Austrian economists (Hayek, Mises) against Marxist planning.
- [Girard](rene-girard.md) took seriously Marx's materialist framing but thought the mimetic dimension was more fundamental than the economic.
