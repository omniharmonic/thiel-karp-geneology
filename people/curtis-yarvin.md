# Curtis Yarvin (Mencius Moldbug)

**(b. 1973)**

American software engineer (Urbit), blogger, and political theorist. Under the pen name *Mencius Moldbug* (2007–2014), wrote the foundational texts of [neoreaction](../schools/neoreaction.md) (NRx), the post-2008 internet-right political philosophy that rejects democratic modernity and argues for a form of monarchical / corporate governance.

## Core claims

- **Democracy is illegitimate and dysfunctional.** Its outcomes are produced by an unaccountable permanent class (the "Cathedral" — universities, press, NGOs, bureaucracy) that sets ideological direction while politicians theatrically compete beneath it.
- **The Cathedral** is a soft-theocracy: a distributed mechanism for manufacturing consensus within the progressive mainstream, analogous in function (though not form) to an established church.
- **Exit, not voice.** Voluntary associations and neo-monarchical polities ("patchwork") offer the only realistic reform: atomize governance into small, exit-available, market-competitive units.
- **CEO-as-sovereign.** The well-run corporation is Yarvin's operating model for governance. A "hard reset" (RAGE: Retire All Government Employees) is preferable to incremental reform.

## Influences

- [Schmitt](carl-schmitt.md) — sovereignty, the critique of liberalism
- Thomas Carlyle — on heroes and strong leadership
- James Burnham (*The Managerial Revolution*) — on the managerial class
- Austrian economics (Mises, Rothbard) — libertarian substrate
- [Heidegger](martin-heidegger.md), Hans-Hermann Hoppe — occasional references
- [Nietzsche](friedrich-nietzsche.md) — ambient background

## Relevance to Thiel

[Thiel](peter-thiel.md) has read Yarvin, hosted Yarvin, funded companies (Urbit, Tlon) Yarvin was part of, and occasionally said things that echo Yarvin-flavored Schmitt. Thiel's 2009 Cato essay — "I no longer believe that freedom and democracy are compatible" — is widely read as partially Yarvin-influenced. J.D. Vance has explicitly cited Yarvin. The RAGE / hard-reset image has shown up in Vance-adjacent and DOGE-adjacent discourse in 2024–2025.
