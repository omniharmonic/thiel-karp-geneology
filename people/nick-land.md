# Nick Land

**(b. 1962)**

British philosopher. Former lecturer at the University of Warwick (1987–1998), where with Sadie Plant he co-founded the **Cybernetic Culture Research Unit** (CCRU) — the late-1990s incubator of what became *accelerationism*. After an academic breakdown, moved to Shanghai (2002–), where he continued to write as a blogger, essayist, and provocateur. The essay **"The Dark Enlightenment"** (2012) is his closest approach to [neoreaction](../schools/neoreaction.md) proper — a long manifesto reading [Yarvin](curtis-yarvin.md)'s Moldbug corpus as the beginning of a new right-wing philosophy.

## Core claims

- **Capitalism is the engine of acceleration.** Not a social form that can be reformed; an intelligence-generating, self-cybernetic process that dissolves all fixed orders through markets, technology, and abstraction.
- **Right-accelerationism.** Against the socialist-accelerationist left (Srnicek, Williams): the acceleration is already capital's native motion. Political projects that try to steer it toward human emancipation are drag on the process. Let it rip.
- **Anti-humanism.** The human is a transitional form. Whatever emerges after — machine intelligence, post-human, something stranger — is what the process is for.
- **Bitcoin and crypto** are read as instantiations of an exit from democratic political control.

## Influences

- Gilles Deleuze and Félix Guattari (*Anti-Oedipus*) — the founding text of Land's early work
- Georges Bataille — excess, sovereignty, general economy
- [Nietzsche](friedrich-nietzsche.md) — constantly
- [Heidegger](martin-heidegger.md) — technology, enframing
- Cybernetics (Norbert Wiener, Gregory Bateson, Gordon Pask)
- [Yarvin](curtis-yarvin.md) — accepted as a kindred spirit in the 2010s

## Relevance to Thiel

Land's acceleration-as-destiny reading of capital resonates with [Thiel](peter-thiel.md)'s worry about technological stagnation — with the difference that Thiel believes we need more acceleration and Land thinks the question is already moot. Thielian and Landian diagnoses of the present often agree even when the cosmology is very different.
