# Sigmund Freud

**(1856–1939)**

Founder of psychoanalysis. The thinker whose mapping of the unconscious, repression, the Oedipus complex, the drives, and the intra-psychic conflict between id, ego, and superego became indispensable for 20th-century continental thought.

## Influence on this vault

- **To Karp:** via the Frankfurt School's systematic integration of Freud into social theory. Adorno's *Authoritarian Personality*, Marcuse's *Eros and Civilization*, and Fromm's *Escape from Freedom* all work within this frame. Karp's dissertation on aggression is a Freud-Parsons-Habermas synthesis.
- **To Thiel:** via [Girard](rene-girard.md), who defined his work against Freud while retaining structural elements. Girard's *Violence and the Sacred* is in deep dialogue with *Totem and Taboo*. The Oedipus complex is, for Girard, a mimetic-rivalry phenomenon that Freud mis-diagnosed as sexual.

## Key works

- *The Interpretation of Dreams* (1900)
- *Totem and Taboo* (1913)
- *Beyond the Pleasure Principle* (1920)
- *The Ego and the Id* (1923)
- *Civilization and Its Discontents* (1930)
- *Moses and Monotheism* (1939)
