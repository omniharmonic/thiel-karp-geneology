# Shared Upstream Ancestors

*Essay 2 of 5 in the synthesis sequence. See [the-central-tension](the-central-tension.md) for the framing question.*

---

## The observation

If you read the vault's person-profiles carefully, you notice that three figures appear in the "influenced by" list of major figures on *both* the Thiel branch and the Karp branch:

- [Hegel](../people/gwf-hegel.md) — via [Kojève](../people/alexandre-kojeve.md) to [Girard](../people/rene-girard.md) (Thiel); via Marx and the Frankfurt tradition to [Habermas](../people/jurgen-habermas.md) (Karp)
- [Nietzsche](../people/friedrich-nietzsche.md) — via [Bloom](../people/allan-bloom.md), [Strauss](../people/leo-strauss.md), and Girard (Thiel); via [Adorno](../people/theodor-adorno.md) and the *[Dialectic of Enlightenment](../concepts/dialectic-of-enlightenment.md)* (Karp)
- [Heidegger](../people/martin-heidegger.md) — direct influence on Strauss, Kojève, Marcuse, Apel, and Habermas, and therefore on every figure downstream of them

This is not coincidence. Any serious 20th-century thinker working on modernity, reason, politics, or religion had to reckon with these three. The interesting question is *how differently* each tradition reckoned with them.

## Hegel: one dialectic, two receptions

Both traditions inherit the [master–slave dialectic](../concepts/master-slave-dialectic.md). But:

- **Girard/Thiel reception:** the struggle is *for desire*, not for recognition; the master-slave structure is the mimetic-doubles structure; history is not the realization of mutual recognition but the escalation of mimetic rivalry; the escalation is only contained by the [scapegoat mechanism](../concepts/scapegoat-mechanism.md) and, eventually, unmasked by the Gospel. The Hegelian trajectory is not emancipatory progress but apocalyptic intensification.

- **Frankfurt/Karp reception:** the struggle is for recognition, which modern institutions can in principle realize; ethical life (*Sittlichkeit*), now re-grounded in [communicative action](../concepts/communicative-action.md) rather than in state metaphysics, remains the goal. The Hegelian trajectory is emancipatory, differentiated, and still ongoing.

Same dialectic, opposite prognoses.

## Nietzsche: diagnostician vs. prophet

Both traditions accept Nietzsche's diagnosis of modernity — the [will to power](../concepts/will-to-power.md), the genealogy of morality, the death of God, the exhaustion of egalitarian morals. Both traditions reject the vulgar-Nazi Nietzsche. But:

- **Thiel's Nietzsche** (via Bloom and Girard): diagnostician of modern nihilism; the figure whose will-to-power authorizes founder ambition; but also the anti-Christian whom the Gospel has already refuted. Thiel holds Nietzsche as the one who *saw* the problem and *missed* the solution.

- **Karp's Nietzsche** (via Adorno and Horkheimer): diagnostician of instrumental reason, of the hidden violence in Enlightenment; a resource for critique of bourgeois rationalism; but not a model for post-Enlightenment conduct. The Frankfurt School absorbs Nietzsche's tools while refusing his aristocratic politics.

Same diagnosis, different therapies.

## Heidegger: the inescapable antagonist

Both traditions are saturated with Heidegger, and both traditions are wary of him.

- **Strauss** attended Heidegger's lectures and defined his mature work against Heidegger's historicism. But the Straussian posture — that the tradition has forgotten something essential that must be recovered — is structurally Heideggerian.

- **[Kojève](../people/alexandre-kojeve.md)** overlays a Heideggerian existentialist frame on Hegel; his Girardian reception preserves this.

- **[Marcuse](../people/herbert-marcuse.md)** studied with Heidegger and never fully exited the phenomenological frame, even while politically opposed.

- **[Apel](../people/karl-otto-apel.md)** started inside Heideggerian phenomenology before breaking toward pragmatism.

- **[Habermas](../people/jurgen-habermas.md)** had his political awakening in 1953 over Heidegger's unapologetic republication of *Introduction to Metaphysics*; his career can be read as a sustained effort to exit Heideggerian thought-shapes while preserving the phenomenological achievements.

Both traditions are post-Heideggerian in the sense that they know they cannot simply ignore him and cannot simply accept him. Where they diverge is on *what* of Heidegger to keep.

## The structural consequence

Thiel and Karp can talk to each other because their traditions share a grammar and share upstream ancestors. When Thiel speaks of the "forgetting of being" or the "return of political theology," he is using vocabulary Karp has encountered inside his own education, even if Karp's tradition has different commitments about what to do with it. When Karp speaks of "system colonizing lifeworld" or "instrumental reason," he is using vocabulary Thiel has encountered in Girard's and Strauss's citations, even if Thiel's tradition metabolizes it differently.

This is what makes their friendship *arguably coherent*: it is an argument inside a shared philosophical inheritance, not a shouting match across incommensurable worlds.

The argument is, however, real. The shared vocabulary does not produce agreement; it produces a productive disagreement in which both parties know what the other is actually claiming.

## A final note on sovereignty and disaster

One observation worth making: when you trace all three lines (Hegel, Nietzsche, Heidegger) to their contemporary extensions, you end up at the same problem that currently animates the vault's primary subjects — the problem of *sovereignty under crisis*.

- For Thiel-via-Schmitt: the state of exception, the decisive moment when normal politics fails
- For Karp-via-Habermas: the legitimation crisis of modern democracies, the colonization of publicity by system logic
- For both via Heidegger: the question of technology as enframing, the forgetting of being, the planetary emergency

These are, from two directions, the same question posed to different answers: what happens when the institutions of modernity cannot stabilize themselves against the forces they have unleashed?

Palantir, if you squint, is a specific answer to that question.
